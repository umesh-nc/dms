Why single database beats federated instances: William Andrews gives to Cathedral Parish and to St. Michael's capital campaign. In a federated model he has two records in two databases — creating the exact duplicate problem the household model was designed to prevent. In the single-database scoped model, William has one Individual record. Cathedral Parish sees his gifts to their funds. St. Michael's sees his gifts to their campaign. The diocese sees everything. No sync. No deduplication. One source of truth.
Single database · four access scopes
Every Atlas user is assigned a scope that determines which records they can see and edit. Scopes are enforced at the query layer — every database query automatically appends a WHERE clause filtering by the user's DioceseID and/or ParishID.

Phase 3
Months 18–30
Future
Atlas Parish Edition — standalone SKU
Atlas Parish Edition: a standalone offering for parishes not part of a Diocesan Atlas umbrella
Priced as a separate SKU below the Diocese tier — lighter feature set, single-parish scope
Upgrade path: when a diocese adopts Diocesan Atlas, standalone Parish Edition instances roll up automatically — data migrates into the diocesan database, no re-entry required
Parish Edition includes: household model, GiveCentral integration, batch entry, basic reports, event management, and Guru AI (query own-parish data only)
Parish Edition excludes: moves management pipeline, wealth screening, planned giving module, cross-parish reporting, diocesan hierarchy — all Diocese tier features
Phase 4
Months 30+
Vision
Multi-diocese administration — National Catholic organisations
A single Atlas instance spanning multiple dioceses — for Catholic national organisations, religious orders, and Catholic Charities networks
The Diocese entity already supports this — DioceseID is in the model from Phase 1. This phase is primarily an access control and billing extension
Each diocese remains its own billing account. A national admin role gets a cross-diocese read-only view for aggregate reporting
