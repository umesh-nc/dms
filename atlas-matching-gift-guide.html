If the employer record doesn't exist yet
Go to Records → New → Organisation. Set Org type to "Employer / Corporation". Enter the company name exactly as it will appear on receipts. Link it to the employee's record via the Employer field on the individual record. Do this before opening batch entry.
Tip — link employer on the employee record first
On James O'Brien's record, confirm the Employer field points to Acme Corporation. This link is what lets Atlas suggest the matching gift automatically when posting and pre-populate the employer on the second gift row.

What "matching gift eligible" does in Atlas
Flagging this row as match-eligible links the personal gift to the employer match when you post the second row. It also enables match tracking in reports — you can see total matched giving per employer and per employee across all campaigns.
3
Post the employer's matching gift
This is a completely separate gift record. The donor is Acme Corporation — not James O'Brien. Hard credit and tax receipt both go to the company.
Most common mistake — wrong donor on Row 2
Never record the matching gift on James O'Brien's record. The match comes from the employer's bank account and the receipt must go to the employer. Using the employee's record for both inflates their giving total with money they didn't actually give, and creates a false tax receipt obligation.

Diocese LTG policy for soft credits — where to configure
Go to Atlas Settings → Gift policies → Soft credit LTG inclusion. The most common setting is "Yes — include matching gift soft credits in donor LTG." Confirm with your development director before your first matching gift batch. This setting affects recognition thresholds, major gift qualification, and invitation lists.

Tax receipts — who gets what

Never do this
Do not add "$500 matched by Acme Corporation" to James O'Brien's tax receipt — it makes it look like he is claiming a deduction for money Acme paid. His receipt must only show the $500 he personally gave. The IRS will flag a receipt that includes amounts not paid by the recipient.
Optional — stewardship note to James
You may send James a separate non-tax stewardship message: "Your employer matched your gift, doubling your impact to $1,000 this year." Great relationship communication — just make sure it contains no deductible amount language and is clearly not a tax receipt.
