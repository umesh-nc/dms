Strategy considerations
How to sequence the 60-client opportunity and address the key objections at each stage.
Existing GC clients first
Lead with the 60 existing clients. They trust GiveCentral, already see the platform's value, and the bundle discount creates a clear financial incentive to consolidate.
–Position Atlas as the CRM layer that was always missing
–No new vendor relationship — just expanding what they have
–Bundle discount removes the "why pay more" objection
–Their GC transaction history pre-populates Atlas on day one
Raiser's Edge displacement
RE NXT scales to $40–80K+ for large dioceses. Atlas competes on native giving integration, diocese-specific hierarchy, and Guru AI — things RE cannot match.
–Lead with the RE annual cost comparison in every proposal
–Migration service removes the switching cost objection
–RE has no native GiveCentral integration — ever
–Diocese clients hate paying for two systems that don't talk
Migration as a profit centre
Migration is not a cost of sale — it's a service line. RE to Atlas migrations are repeatable and can be systematised across 60 clients.
–Build the RE → Atlas import toolkit once, amortise across 60
–Offer a fixed-price guarantee to reduce client anxiety
–Use early migrations as case studies for later conversions
–Margin improves dramatically by client 10
Pipeline revenue potential
At mid-tier pricing the 60-client opportunity is substantial even at conservative conversion rates.
–60 clients × $65K avg = $3.9M ARR at full conversion
–Migration: 60 × $34K avg = $2.04M one-time revenue
–20% Yr 1 conversion = $780K ARR + $408K migration
–GC + Atlas clients churn significantly less than single-product
