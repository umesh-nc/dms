Hard credit
The legal donor — the entity whose funds were transferred. Receives the tax receipt. Counted in revenue totals. Appears on acknowledgement letters with deductible amount.
Soft credit
A relationship or influence attribution. Never generates a tax receipt. Never counted in revenue. Used for LTG tracking, stewardship, portfolio reporting, and giving history display only.

Key rule: Soft credits must never appear on tax receipts and must never be summed in revenue totals. They exist solely for stewardship, relationship management, and lifetime giving calculations.  ·  DAF vs. QCD: A DAF grant: hard credit to the DAF org, soft credit to the donor, no receipt to donor. A QCD: hard credit directly to the donor, no soft credit needed, but requires a QCD-specific acknowledgement letter — not a standard tax receipt.
